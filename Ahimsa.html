Values From Vedas

Values Of Ahimsa:

Ahimsa refers to non-injury to the sacrificer himself. The Ahimsa doctrine is a late Vedic era development in Brahmanical culture. In Jainism, ahimsa is the standard by which all actions are judged.

Ahimsa provide us with values such as:

  • the ethical principle of not causing harm to other living things,
  • Hindu and Buddhist doctrine of refraining from harming any living being.
